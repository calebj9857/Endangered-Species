Species That Need Our Help

Critically Endangered
Big Cat · Russia & China

Amur Leopard

~128–130 remaining

Native to the Primorye region of southeastern Russia, the Amur leopard is one of the world's rarest wild cats, hunted for its striking spotted coat.

Critically Endangered
Megafauna · Indonesia

Javan Rhino

~50–81 remaining

Surviving only in Ujung Kulon National Park, the Javan rhinoceros is the rarest large mammal on Earth, threatened by habitat loss and poaching.

Endangered
Primate · Central Africa

Mountain Gorilla

~1,063 remaining

Found in the volcanic mountains of Central Africa, these gentle giants face threats from habitat encroachment, disease, and civil conflict.

Endangered
Big Cat · Central Asia

Snow Leopard

~4,000–6,500 remaining

The ghost of the mountains, this elusive cat roams the high peaks of the Himalayas, facing threats from poaching and climate change.

Critically Endangered
Megafauna · Indonesia

Sumatran Elephant

~2,000–2,800 remaining

Smaller than their mainland cousins, these elephants once roamed Sumatra's lowland forests. Over half their population has been lost in one generation.

Critically Endangered
Cetacean · Gulf of California

Vaquita

~6–10 remaining

The world's most endangered marine mammal, the tiny vaquita porpoise vanishes due to illegal gill-net fishing in Mexico's Gulf of California.
